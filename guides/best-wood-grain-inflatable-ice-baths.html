How to choose

Temperature protocol matters more than the tub. The research on cold water immersion points to 50-59°F (10-15°C) for 10-20 minutes as the sweet spot for recovery benefits. Without a chiller, you're at the mercy of your ice budget. A water thermometer is non-negotiable.

Ice cost adds up without a chiller. Filling a 150+ gallon tub to 50°F with ice costs $15-30 per session depending on your starting water temperature. A water chiller ($300-600) pays for itself in 2-3 months of daily use. Budget for it if you're serious.

Location determines the setup. Wood-grain aesthetics demand an outdoor space where the tub can be a feature — deck, patio, backyard. These tubs are 5-6 feet in diameter inflated. Measure your space before buying and allow clearance on all sides.

Cold plunging after water sports amplifies recovery. A post-surf, post-SUP, or post-kayak cold plunge reduces inflammation and muscle soreness faster than passive rest. The combination of water sports and cold therapy is increasingly supported by sports science research.
