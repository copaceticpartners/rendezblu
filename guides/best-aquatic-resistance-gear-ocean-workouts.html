How to choose

Water magnifies resistance dramatically. Moving your arm through water generates 12-14 times more resistance than moving it through air. This means light aquatic resistance tools produce strength training effects that would require much heavier equipment on land. Start lighter than you think you need.

Chest depth is the sweet spot for most exercises. Chest-deep water keeps your feet on the ground for stability while submerging your upper body for resistance. Waist-deep reduces resistance significantly. Deeper than chest and you lose footing stability. Most ocean resistance circuits work best in chest-deep water.

Low impact doesn't mean low intensity. Water workouts are famously low-impact on joints but can be extremely high-intensity cardiovascularly. Your heart rate elevation during aqua jogging or ocean shadowboxing rivals land-based HIIT. Don't underestimate the intensity because the impact is absent.

Combine tools for a complete circuit. A complete ocean workout circuit: 5 minutes aqua jogging with TYR belt (cardio) → 3 rounds of shadowboxing with Speedo gloves (upper body) → 4x25m swim sprints with FINIS parachute (power) → cool down. Total time: 45 minutes, zero equipment weight to carry home.

Our bottom line

Add the FINIS Parachute when you want serious power development. The TYR belt unlocks deep water running which is genuinely underrated as a low-impact cardio alternative.
