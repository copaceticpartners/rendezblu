A rash guard does three things: blocks UV radiation, prevents board rash, and keeps you warm in and out of the water. How to choose

Long sleeve vs short sleeve. Long sleeve offers more UV protection and slightly more warmth in the water. Short sleeve gives more arm freedom and feels less restrictive on hot days. For most water sports, long sleeve is the right call — the UV protection is significant over a full season.

UPF rating matters. UPF 50+ blocks 98% of UV radiation. A white cotton t-shirt provides roughly UPF 5. The difference over a summer of daily beach sessions is the difference between a tan and serious sun damage. Don't skip the UPF rating.

Flatlock stitching prevents rash. Standard stitching creates raised seams that abrade skin during repeated motion — exactly the kind of chafing that gives rash guards their name. Flatlock stitching lays flat against the skin. Always check the seam construction.

Size to your measurements not your shirt size. Rash guards should fit snug — loose rash guards bunch up under you on the board and against the seat of a kayak. Most brands have detailed sizing charts based on chest and height. Size down if you're between sizes.
