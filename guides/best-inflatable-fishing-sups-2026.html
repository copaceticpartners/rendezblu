How to choose

Width is stability — minimum 32" for fishing. Casting, fighting fish, and landing a catch all require you to move on the board. Under 32" wide and you're fighting balance on every movement. The BOTE at 38" is about as stable as an inflatable gets. Don't sacrifice width for speed on a fishing board.

Standing visibility changes your fishing. From a seated kayak you see the surface. From a standing SUP you see down into the water column — fish, structure, grass beds. In clear water this visibility advantage is significant. It's why serious sight-fishing guides are switching to SUP platforms.

Casting radius is 360°. On a kayak you're casting around a boat. On a SUP you have 360° of unobstructed casting radius — no hull, no seat back, no partner in front of you. For fly fishing and sight casting, this is a fundamental advantage.

Electric trolling motors transform the range. A trolling motor mount on the Sea Eagle FishSUP turns a human-powered platform into a motorized skiff. For covering large flats or fighting wind on the way back, this option dramatically expands what a fishing SUP can do.

Our bottom line
For serious anglers, the BOTE Rackham Aero is worth every dollar — it's the only board designed exclusively for fishing rather than adapted for it. For anglers who want motor capability, Sea Eagle FishSUP. Best value with kayak seat conversion? Highpi. The fishing SUP category has genuinely matured — these are real fishing platforms, not novelty boards.
