What you need to know before buying

eFoils are not like other water sports purchases. This is a $6,000–$16,000 investment that requires service, parts, and ideally a dealer relationship. Buy from a brand with US dealer support and a real warranty. Lift and Fliteboard are the most established in the US market.

Try before you buy. Almost every eFoil brand has lesson programs and demo days. You can learn to ride in a single session with proper instruction. Budget $100–$200 for a lesson before committing to a purchase — it will confirm whether this is for you and which board feels right.

Wing size determines your learning curve. Larger wings (2000+ cm²) create more lift at lower speeds — much easier for beginners. Smaller wings require more speed to generate lift but give you more maneuverability once you know what you're doing. Start big.

Battery and charging logistics matter. A 90-minute session is typical. Charging takes 2–3 hours. If you want all-day riding you need a second battery or a fast charger. Factor this into your total cost of ownership.

Our bottom line
If budget isn't the constraint, the Lift LIFT5 is the best eFoil money can buy — the assembly system and battery alone justify the premium. For beginners, the Fliteboard AIR's soft deck reduces the pain of learning and the performance is genuinely impressive. Best value in the category right now is the Waydoo EVO Pro Plus — Flight Assist stabilization at half the price of a Lift is hard to argue with.
