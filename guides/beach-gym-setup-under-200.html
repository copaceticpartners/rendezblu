2. Sandbag — ~$45

// must-have #2
Durable Workout Sandbag
Fill it at the beach. Squat, lunge, carry, throw.
A sandbag is the ultimate beach gym piece of equipment. It's literally made for the environment. Buy it empty, fill it when you arrive, dump it when you leave. No transporting heavy weight. Look for a reinforced nylon bag with multiple handles for different grip positions. A 50lb filled sandbag on the beach will humble you fast — squats, carries, cleans, and shouldering movements are all brutally effective on sand.

3. Jump Rope — ~$15

// must-have #3
Crossrope / Speed Jump Rope
10 minutes of double-unders on sand is cardio you won't forget
Jump rope on sand is a completely different experience than on a gym floor. The surface is unstable, your landing absorbs differently, and your calves will be destroyed in a good way. A basic speed rope works fine — look for a ball-bearing handle for smooth rotation and an adjustable cable length. Keep it simple, under $20, and focus on the movement.
