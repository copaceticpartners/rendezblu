What to look for

Water rating is everything. 5ATM means 50 meters. 10ATM means 100 meters. For SUP and ocean swimming, 5ATM is fine. For surfing and diving, go 10ATM minimum. "Water resistant" with no ATM rating means nothing.

GPS accuracy matters on water. Standard GPS loses accuracy on open water because there are no buildings or structures to help triangulate position. Multi-band GPS like Garmin's system is significantly more accurate for paddle tracking.

Battery life is critical. A full day on the water with GPS active drains most watches fast. Look for 10+ hours of active GPS battery minimum. Solar charging is a game changer for multi-day trips.

Salt water matters. Not all watches explicitly state saltwater compatibility. Garmin and Apple Watch Ultra are both confirmed saltwater safe. For cheaper options, rinse with fresh water after every ocean session to extend life.

Our bottom line
For most people on the water, the Garmin Instinct 2 Solar is the right call. Solar charging removes battery anxiety, the 100m rating handles everything, and the $299 price is fair for what you get. Apple Watch Ultra 2 is better if you're already deep in the Apple ecosystem and want the premium experience. Budget option? Fitbit Sense 2 at $199 handles SUP and ocean swimming without issue.
