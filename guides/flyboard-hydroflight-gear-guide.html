How to choose

You need a personal watercraft (PWC). Flyboards don't come with a jet ski — they attach to one. You either own a PWC or rent access to one. Most flyboard operators at tourist destinations include the PWC in the package price.

Start with a lesson. Every serious flyboard vendor offers instruction. Your first session on a flyboard will involve falling — a lot. With good instruction you'll be hovering in 20–30 minutes. Without it, the learning curve is significantly longer and more painful.

The PWC operator controls your power. In the standard setup, the person on the jet ski controls the throttle — they control how high and fast you go. A wireless remote upgrade lets you control your own throttle, which is significantly more fun once you're comfortable.

Gear up properly. Wetsuit or impact vest, helmet, and a leash are non-negotiable for flyboarding. The falls happen fast and from height. Protect yourself.

Our bottom line
For most people the Flyboard Pro Series is the right purchase — the weight reduction and independent foot pads are real upgrades over the Legend. If you're more of a surfer at heart, the Hoverboard gives you a horizontal experience that's completely different from the vertical flight of the Flyboard. Either way, take a lesson first. Seriously.
