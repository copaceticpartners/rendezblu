How to choose

Fit determines whether you'll wear it. The best PFD is the one you actually put on. If it's uncomfortable, bulky, or restricts your movement, you'll leave it in your car. Try it on with a paddle stroke motion before buying. It should move with you.

Activity type changes what you need. Kayaking needs a low-back design. SUP benefits from paddle-specific cuts. Surfing needs minimal bulk. Don't buy a boating PFD for paddling — the difference in usability is significant.

USCG Type III is the standard for water sports. Type III is designed for calm water activities where quick rescue is likely. It provides 15.5 lbs of buoyancy and is approved for most recreational water sports. Type V is for high-risk activities. Type I is for offshore use.

Children need their own sizing. Never use adult PFDs for children. Child PFDs are sized by weight and designed to turn a face-down unconscious child face-up. Adult PFDs do not have this feature. Always buy age and weight appropriate PFDs for children.
