How to choose

Bluetooth doesn't work underwater. This is why standard Bluetooth earbuds don't work for swimming. Water blocks the signal completely. All serious swim headphones use onboard MP3 storage for underwater use and Bluetooth for land. Always check for built-in storage before buying.

Bone conduction vs in-ear for water sports. Bone conduction transmits sound through your skull bones — your ear canal stays open, which is safer for open water swimming (you can still hear boat traffic and other hazards). In-ear designs block ambient sound entirely. For ocean use, bone conduction is the safer choice.

IP68 is the minimum for submersion. IPX7 means splashproof. IP68 means actually submersible. For lap swimming and diving under waves, you need IP68 minimum. Check the specific depth and duration rating — some IP68 ratings only cover 1 meter for 30 minutes.

Sync your music before you hit the water. Bluetooth headphones need to sync their MP3 library from your phone before you enter the water. Build a dedicated swim playlist, sync it, then leave your phone on deck. The syncing process takes a few minutes and varies by brand.
