How to choose

Drop-stitch floors are non-negotiable for performance. A drop-stitch floor uses thousands of threads connecting the top and bottom layers, allowing ultra-high pressure inflation. The result is a rigid, hardboard-like floor. Without it, the kayak flexes underfoot and feels like an air mattress.

Self-bailing ports matter for rivers. For flatwater, no self-bailing needed. For rivers and rapids, self-bailing ports drain water automatically so it doesn't accumulate in the cockpit. The Driftsun Rover has 8. For whitewater, this is essential.

Weight capacity affects stability. A kayak rated at 300 lbs paddled by a 150 lb person will be more stable than one at maximum capacity. Leave 30-40% of capacity unused for the most stable ride.

Multi-chamber designs are safer. If one air chamber is punctured on a single-chamber kayak, you sink. Multi-chamber kayaks maintain floatation even with one chamber compromised. Always check how many independent chambers a kayak has.
