Best Surfboards for Beginners
(2026 Guide)

Updated April 2026 · 7 min read · rendezblu.com

Your first surfboard should be big, thick, and forgiving — not what you see pros riding. More volume = more paddling power = more waves caught = faster progression. The best beginner surfboards are soft tops, longboards, and mid-lengths that let you practice technique without fighting your equipment.

How to choose

Volume is your friend. More volume = more buoyancy = more paddle speed = more waves = more practice. Every wave you catch as a beginner is irreplaceable learning. Get a board with high volume and catch a hundred waves before worrying about style.

Soft tops don't hurt when they hit you. Fiberglass boards are sharp-railed weapons when they bounce back in the whitewater and hit you in the face. Foam boards are forgiving. As a beginner you will get hit by your board repeatedly. Foam makes this survivable.

Length over width for paddle power. Longer boards have more surface area paddling through water. An 8'6" will catch waves a 6'0" would miss entirely. Don't buy the board you want to ride eventually — buy the board that makes you a better surfer right now.

Take a lesson first. One two-hour lesson with a qualified instructor is worth three months of self-teaching. Learn to read the lineup, time your paddle, and pop up correctly before developing bad habits that take years to unlearn.

Our bottom line
The Wavestorm 8' is the iconic first board for good reason — it works, it's available, and it's under $200. When you're ready to step up, Catch Surf Odysea Log for soft top performance or South Bay Board Co for a more refined feel. If you have board sport experience and want to progress faster, start on a midlength and accept the steeper learning curve.
